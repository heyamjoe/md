Monde

Monde is a grade ten secondary learner at Cresent Secondary school in Kamwala , in Lusaka District. She was raped a few months ago and she is physically and psychologically scarred. She doesn’t have anyone to confide in because at home her mother and father are on the verge of divorce. She is currently staying with her auntie and uncle and life is so painful to her. Her feelings are bottling up so much that she fears she can implode hence she chooses to start talking to an imaginary friend called Diary.

Monde’s Diary is a seven party series with the first title as Monde’s Diary; My Christmas Wish and with the second title being Monde’s Diary; Drama, dram… Drama! And with many others to be released.

Vision

The vision of Monde’s Diary is to build the destinies of primary and secondary learners through mentorship and character building. Guiding primary and secondary learners into fulfilling their dreams and making them achieve their greatest potentials. In the same vein fighting juvenile delinquency and reducing the crime rates of our societies.

Objectives

  • Preaching the gospel of Jesus Christ
  • Advocating for intellectual and academic brilliance
  • Providing a platform of innovation and horning of skills and talents so that after secondary school our learner become high standing members of society

Activities

  1. a. Monde’s Diary reading clubs; these are one hour sessions that the learners have after class hosted by Chaplains as the read excerpts from the book and asks questions as they learn from Monde’s Diary
  2. b. Monde Mentor Me Party; these are Saturday parties that are hosted by the chaplains with four aspects;
    1. Intercession
    2. Lessons from the book
    3. Word exhortation
    4. Open arts (Poetry, drama et cetera)
    5. Praise and worship
    6. Prize winning
    7. Food

The Monde’s Diary Family

Every learner that buy’s a copy of the book becomes a registered member of the Monde’s Diary Christian family and gets opportunities of being sponsored into higher education training.
